Welcome to Sofia

Sofia is the heart of Bulgaria – a city where ancient history from Thracian and Roman times meets the modern European pulse.

Situated at the foot of Vitosha Mountain, Sofia offers a unique combination of easy access to nature and rich urban culture. With its magnificent temples, cozy cafes, and vibrant rhythm, Sofia is a city that always has something new to reveal.

As one of Europe’s oldest capitals, Sofia has been continuously inhabited for over 7,000 years. Today it is a dynamic, modern city with a population of around 1.3 million, serving as Bulgaria’s political, economic, and cultural centre.

Sightseeing

Top Attractions

Must-see landmarks within walking distance of Grand Hotel Sofia

Alexander Nevski Cathedral

One of the largest Eastern Orthodox cathedrals in the world, this iconic Neo-Byzantine masterpiece is Sofia’s most famous landmark. Built in the early 20th century, it can hold 10,000 people and features stunning golden domes.

National Art Gallery

Housed in the former Royal Palace, the gallery contains Bulgaria’s largest collection of fine art. Located right next to the City Garden and steps from Grand Hotel Sofia.

Ivan Vazov National Theatre

Bulgaria’s oldest and most authoritative theatre, situated in the beautiful City Garden. Its Neo-Classical facade is one of Sofia’s most photographed landmarks, just steps from the hotel.

Vitosha Boulevard

Sofia’s main pedestrian shopping street, lined with numerous shops, unique boutiques, local artisan products, cafes and restaurants. A lively promenade with views of Vitosha Mountain.

Ancient Serdica Ruins

Discover the remains of the ancient Roman city of Serdica, found during metro construction. The open-air archaeological complex at Serdika metro station showcases Sofia’s layered history.

Vitosha Mountain

A natural park right at the doorstep of the city. Vitosha offers hiking trails, stunning panoramic views, and fresh mountain air – all within a short drive from the city centre.

Useful Information

Things to know before visiting Sofia

Currency

Euro (€). Bulgaria is a member of the Eurozone. Credit cards are widely accepted.

Language

Bulgarian (Cyrillic script). English is widely spoken in hotels, restaurants, and tourist areas.

October Weather

Average temperatures of 8–18°C in early October. Bring a light jacket for evenings.

Electricity

230V, 50Hz. European standard two-pin plugs (Type C and F).
